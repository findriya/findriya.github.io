How to Balancing School Life with Family and Work

Two Tips

Support System at Home

I couldn't have made it through my undergraduate program without the support of my children, my husband, my parents, family and friends. There were times I wanted to quit, sometimes I'd take a quiz and call up my mom knowing full well I probably failed. She'd talk to me and tell me not to give up.

Alaska Native Science & Engineering Program (ANSEP) was my home away from home and I had core study groups assigned and put together by the program.
It helped knowing I had a study group to work out problems I didn't understand. It also forced me to make friends, something well, I've always had a hard time doing. I am not one with 20 to 30 friends, I have three or four real close friends, that I can pick up the phone and call after 2 weeks of not talking and feel like we just spoke yesterday.

Be Realistic About How Much You Can Handle

Being real on scholarship essays about my juggling mommy-hood, part-time work, and full-time school did help get me some extra funding early in my undergraduate degree program. So there's something to be said about it.

Also, I was reminded a couple of times when I was being all woe-me, I'm so tired that there are successful Alaska Native women from our region that had to work, go to school and be a mom as a single parent. My hats are off to those women! Thank you for being great role models, you know who you are.

Taken from an article written by Stefanie M. Armstrong. April 10, 2016.
